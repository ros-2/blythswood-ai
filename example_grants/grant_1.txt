APPLICATION TO THE COMMUNITY CONNECTIONS FUND
Grant Award: £8,500
Date: January 2024

OUR STORY

In 2019, a man named Jeremy discovered something remarkable: his smile could change lives. Not through magic, but through something equally powerful - genuine human connection in an increasingly disconnected world.

Jeremy's journey began on a grey Tuesday morning when he smiled at a woman crying at a bus stop. She later tracked him down to say that simple smile had stopped her from giving up entirely. That moment launched Jeremy's Wonderful Smile Charity, an organization built on the radical belief that sometimes the most powerful intervention is also the simplest: one person showing they care.

Since then, Jeremy has walked over 4,000 miles through Manchester's streets, delivering his gift to anyone who needs it. We've responded to 156 smile emergencies - people at their absolute lowest, reaching out for help. And we've trained 43 others in the art of meaningful smiling, creating ripples of kindness across Northwest England.

But this isn't really about Jeremy's smile. It's about what that smile represents: someone seeing you, acknowledging your humanity, and offering hope without judgment.

THE PROJECT: SMILE TRAINING FOR YOUTH

We want to teach 60 young people (ages 14-18) the power of positive human connection through our Smile Training Workshop program.

Picture this: Sarah, 16, bullied at school, walks past others with eyes down, afraid to make contact. After our workshop, she learns that a genuine smile is both armor and bridge - it protects her confidence while opening doors to connection. Within three weeks, she's made two new friends and reports feeling "less scared of people."

That's what we do. We don't just teach people to smile - we teach them to see the humanity in others and have their own humanity recognized.

Our six-session program covers: the psychology of facial expressions and emotional contagion; overcoming social anxiety through gradual exposure; practical exercises in public spaces (always supervised); processing experiences in supportive group settings; understanding boundaries and authentic connection versus forced cheerfulness; building confidence through documented positive interactions.

Each cohort of 12 young people meets weekly for six weeks. Jeremy leads sessions, supported by a youth worker and volunteer mentors. Participants practice skills in controlled environments before graduating to independent "smile walks" in their communities.

WHY THIS MATTERS

Manchester has the third-highest youth mental health referral rate in the UK. Average wait time for CAMHS: 18 weeks. Our program provides immediate, accessible intervention that doesn't require clinical diagnosis or parental permission (though we encourage family involvement).

More than that, it addresses isolation at its root. We're not treating symptoms - we're rebuilding the social fabric that prevents those symptoms emerging.

Young people today face unprecedented social challenges: social media comparison, pandemic-related isolation, economic anxiety, and fractured community connections. Traditional mental health services are overwhelmed. School-based support is limited. Parents often lack tools to help. Our program fills a crucial gap - teaching practical social connection skills that have immediate, measurable impact.

THE EVIDENCE

Our pilot with 12 young people showed remarkable results: 91% reported increased confidence in social situations; 67% made at least one new meaningful friendship; 83% described feeling "less lonely"; 100% would recommend to peers; Zero dropout rate (remarkable for youth programs).

One participant wrote: "I used to think I was broken because I couldn't talk to people. Jeremy taught me I'm not broken - I just needed practice and permission to be kind."

Another shared: "Before this program, I would panic when someone looked at me. Now I can smile at strangers and it doesn't feel scary. I feel like I have a superpower."

Parents reported positive changes at home: improved family communication, increased willingness to participate in social activities, reduced anxiety symptoms, and greater emotional resilience.

Teachers at participating schools noticed shifts in classroom dynamics: more peer support, reduced bullying incidents, increased participation from previously withdrawn students, and improved overall classroom atmosphere.

HOW THE GRANT HELPS

£8,500 funds five cohorts reaching 60 young people total:

Youth worker fees: £4,000 (professional facilitation, safeguarding, administrative support)
Venue hire: £1,500 (accessible community spaces for six-week programs)
Materials and resources: £800 (workbooks, certificates, reflection journals, visual aids)
Participant expenses: £1,200 (travel costs, healthy snacks, removing financial barriers)
Celebration event: £500 (graduation ceremony, certificates, family inclusion)
Evaluation and impact reporting: £500 (professional assessment, data collection, outcome analysis)

Every young person receives a completion certificate, ongoing access to our community support network, and invitation to become peer mentors for future cohorts - turning recipients into ambassadors who spread skills throughout their schools and communities.

LOOKING AHEAD

This program transforms lives while building an army of kindness. Sixty young people, each touching hundreds of others, create exponential impact. We're not just teaching smiling - we're building the next generation of community connectors.

These young people will carry these skills throughout their lives: into workplaces, relationships, parenting, community leadership. They become ambassadors for connection in a disconnected world.

We're also creating pathway for ongoing engagement: graduates can volunteer as peer mentors, participate in advanced leadership training, and join our youth advisory board to shape future program development. Several pilot participants have already become volunteer smile ambassadors, conducting their own mini-workshops at their schools.

And sometimes, the future of mental health support looks less like clinical interventions and more like teenagers learning that their presence matters, their smile has power, and connection is possible. That's the world we're building, one smile at a time.
